Best-of Tufts

Best-of Tufts was my first rodeo with Ruby on Rails. It allows users to sign up with a Tufts email in order to create and vote on "match-ups" to see what the community favorites are. Check it out here!

  • Client: Personal
  • Date: April 2014
  • Service: Development

Potlucky

Potlucky is a platform for organizing pot-lucks, food shares, picnics, and any other gathering with food. Users can host or attend meals, and can suggest dishes by either creating them or searching Yummly. Users can also claim the things that they will be bringing! You can find Potlucky online here.

  • Client: Personal (GA)
  • Date: June 2014
  • Service: Design & Development

All My Circuits

'All My Circuits' was a collaborative project with other GA students Max Kohl and Julie Soforenko. We set out to make a dating site where programmers could connect with one another based on work experience and dorky interests. While it was a lot of fun mixing nerdism with romanticism, we all learned a valuable lesson by fibbing the git branches and sowing some deep-seeded issues on heroku. Some day we'll get the band back together...

  • Client: General Assembly
  • Date: July 2014
  • Service: Data modeling, back-end and Ajax

Tapper Tracker

Tapper Tracker is an adaptation of a research study I conducted as an undergrad. There are a lot of fascinating properties of human rhythm perception, and this app provides real time data visualization to illustrate some of those properties. Crafted as a game, I plan to re-cast Tapper Tracker as a teaching tool for use by music and music psychology professors. You can find it here.

  • Client: Personal (GA), Aniruddh Patel
  • Date: August 2014
  • Service: Design & Development

Jeremy Carroll

Jeremy is an insanely talented musician, composer, poet and teacher. He's an old friend (and current housemate...gave me my first skateboard, in fact) and when he mentioned wanting a website to host his music channels and lessons, I jumped to help. I threw together a pretty simple blog platform for him to work in, and will keep updating it with his needs. It's like having a client, but if they were the perfect client and occasionally vacuumed. Follow his work here.

  • Client: Jeremy Carroll
  • Date: March 2015
  • Service: Design, Development, & Maintenance
